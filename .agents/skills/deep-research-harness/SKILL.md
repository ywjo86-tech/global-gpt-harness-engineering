# Deep Research Harness

Use this skill when a question requires evidence gathering across multiple source types and a synthesized report.

## Inputs

- Research question
- Decision context
- Source preferences or exclusions
- Time or geography constraints
- Required output format

## Agent Team Pattern

- Research lead: frames the question and evidence plan.
- Web researcher: gathers current web and primary-source material.
- Academic researcher: checks papers, standards, and formal references.
- Community signal analyst: reviews practitioner and community reactions.
- Verification reviewer: cross-checks claims and source quality.
- Synthesis writer: produces the final report.

## Workflow

1. Restate the research question and decision need.
2. Define source categories and acceptance criteria.
3. Gather current web sources and official references.
4. Gather academic, standards, or technical literature when relevant.
5. Review community or practitioner signals separately from verified facts.
6. Cross-check important claims across independent sources.
7. Separate findings, uncertainty, and recommendations.
8. Produce a concise report with source notes and follow-up gaps.

## Outputs

- Research scope
- Source map
- Evidence summary
- Cross-check notes
- Final synthesis report
- Open questions

## Validation

- Important claims have source support.
- Community signals are labeled as signals, not facts.
- Conflicts between sources are called out.
- Recommendations state confidence and uncertainty.
